Search Overview

The CLC allows for viewing and restoring of any documents and emails while still securely communicating through web services across a network.   Once this small application is installed and running, you can then view and restore any document that appears in your search-return viewing grid.  ECM Library uses advanced secure gateway and transfer methods so that you can be reassured that files transfers will be accessed both quickly and securely.

Installing or Launching CLC

To install the CLC, click on the hyperlink “Launch CLC” to install the CLC for the first time.   If your browser gives you any warnings, click on “run anyway”.  The install takes just a few seconds.

The CLC needs to be launched for every new ECM Library session.  Click on “Launch CLC” anytime you want to download and view a document or email.

Viewing or Restoring Documents

Once you done a search and search results appear, you can highlight one or more rows in the search return grid to restore and view these documents.  With your selection highlighted, right-click mouse and select “Restore Selected Content”.  Your documents will transfer to your computer over the network every few seconds with the CLC launched and running on your computer.

Troubleshooting - CLC Not Found?

If your search screen ever shows a red warning that indicates the CLC is not found AND you have the CLC running on your computer, click on “Find CLC” in the lower right hand side of the search screen.   You can also in the CLC under “Folders” click on “find CLC” as additional step to take if the warning does not go away. 

CLC Most Common Functions

1.        The easiest and best way to open up ECM Library in the browser is by launching your CLC first (e.g. using the shortcut on your desktop) and then click on “Open Search Browser”.

2.       The CLC does a check for any requests for documents restores every few seconds.  If you want to see the document or email faster, click on download in the CLC after you have right-clicked mouse and selected “Restore Selected Content”.
