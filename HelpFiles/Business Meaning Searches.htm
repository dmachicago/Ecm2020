For Users and Global Searchers – How Business Meaning Works:

Business meaning is also called FREETEXT in SQL Search queries.   It is a good tool to use when you have a “concept” of what you are looking for, but not sure of specific words or catch phrases.  We suggest using a maximum of three keywords for business meaning with weights.  Otherwise the search may too slow.   

Business Meaning is available in the content and the email search screen but not in the quick/all search screen.

Business meaning automatically includes:

·         Inflectional searches on all words you enter.   E.g. run, running, ran etc.

·         It will look for density or proximity of terms in each document based on the list that is generated in your search. 

If your business has populated the Microsoft Thesaurus, those expansion or substitution words will be automatically used in Business Meaning.  See your administrator for details. 

Checking business for a phrase means “ take my set of keywords and match up meanings of these keywords.   Then search everywhere for concepts that are around these terms.”  It does not search on a phrase per say, but looks at each individual word.  Clicking on business meaning will be far less precise than a non-business meaning search, but it is very powerful because it looks for concepts around keywords.  For example, “business proposal customer”  will look for these words and any similar like words that are grouped close together.  

With a Business Meaning search, the AND, AND NOT and the NEAR search operator will be disregarded. Quotations will be disregarded.  It just looks for the “listing” of words that you have entered.

With Business Meaning, you are creating a list of phrases (with double quotations) or words that will all be searched.  Thesaurus is disabled with Business Meaning due to the too-extensive query that is most often created, slowing down the search geometrically.

Business Meaning searches are best combined with a weighted search.  But please be aware that your search will be slower with these two search tools combined.

Search Timeouts?

If you are running expansive, wide sweeping searches which, by their nature, use many search parameters (e.g. many keywords with business meaning) and your searches timeout so you can’t get your results back, you do have options!

If your search is a content search, try your initial search with less search parameters.  Next, do a reduction search by clicking on “limit to current list” in the content search screen.  This will allow you to add new search parameters one search at a time.

If you are doing a business meaning search, try reducing your parameters by opening up the search assistant and identifying words you need for your search that are more specific. With a little more work your search can be just as effective by using the Search Assistant screen.  Business Meaning searches may behind the scenes be adding too many words for the search based on the Microsoft Thesaurus matches and the inflectional matches.

Generally, look at how you can simplify your search.  For example, it could be that taking off weights or by searching just on emails vs. everything that you get back what you need.

Another option is to contact your ECM Library administrator.  Your administrator may be able to set the enterprise timeout parameter to a longer number of seconds.

For Administrators - Business Meaning Searches:

This is selected to search columns containing character-based data types for values that match the meaning and not just the exact wording of the words in the search condition. When Business Meaning is used, the full-text query engine internally performs the following actions on the “freetext_string”. It assigns each term a weight, and then finds the matches.

·         Separates the string into individual words based on word boundaries (word-breaking).

·         Generates inflectional forms of the words (stemming).

·         Identifies a list of expansions or replacements for the terms based on matches in the thesaurus.
